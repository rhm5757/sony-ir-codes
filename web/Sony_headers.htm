Sony Model Number Headers

 

This is basically a big list of the model number headers (2 or 3 letters) and what types of devices they correspond to.

 

AC – AC adaptor

ACC – accessory pack

AVD – DVD/Receiver combo

 

BC – battery charger

BVW – Betacam player

 

CCD, CDV – camcorder

CDP – CD player

CDX – CD Car Stereo

CFD – Boombox

CMT – small mini system

CSS – camera dock

CVP – Color Video Printer

 

D – CD Walkman

DAV – Dream System

DCC – car battery charger

DCR – Digital Camcorder (DV and DVD-R based)

DHG – HDTV receiver/DVR

DHR – DV (mode 5)

DPP – photo printer

DRN – XM satellite radio tuner

DSC – digital still camera

DSR – DV (mode 4)

DSV – DVD (early?)

DTC – DAT recorder

DV – pro DV camera (device 223)

DVW – digital Betacam equipment

 

EVC – VCR (mode 2)

EVO – TV and VCR (mode 2)

EVS - VCR

 

HDH – projector

HDL – HD (proprietary Sony) laser disc

HDR – HD camcorder

HIL – Muse HD laser disc

HMD – CRT computer monitor

HT – Home Theater package

 

ICF – kitchen radio

INT - WebTV

 

K* - TV

KL, KLV, KP, KV, KW, KWP – color TV

KD – HD CRT TV

KDE – HD plasma TV

KDF – HD rear projection LCD TV

KDS – HD rear projection SXRD TV

KE – plasma TV

KF – rear projection LCD TV

KLV – LCD TV

KP – rear projection CRT TV

KV – standard CRT TV

 

LCS – camera bag

LMD – pro LCD video monitor

LVA – Laser VideoDisc player

LVR – Laser VideoDisc recorder

 

MDP – Laser Disc

MDR - headphones

MDS, MDJ – MiniDisc

MDX – MiniDisc car stereo

MEX – DVD car stereo

MHC – large mini system

MSH, MSX – Memory Stick media

MV – portable DVD player

MVC – Mavica still camera

MVR – still video recorder

MXD – CD/MD combo

MZ – MiniDisc portable

 

NP - battery

NW – network Walkman (flash memory player)

 

PCG – Vaio notebook PC

PCM – pro DAT recorder

PCS – videoconferencing equipment

PCV – Vaio PC

PEG – Sony Clie PDA

PHD – digital photo frame

PS - phonograph

PTM – DAT

PVM – pro video monitor

 

RCD – CD recorder

RDR – DVD recorder

 

SA, SS – speakers

SAS, SAT – Satellite receiver

SBV, SVB? – A/V Switch

SCD – SACD player

SDM – LCD computer monitor

SDP – Surround Processor

SL – Betamax VCR (mode 1)

SLV – VHS VCR (mode 3), also VCR/DVD combos

SPP – cordless phone

STR – receiver

STS – active speakers

STU – TV codes

SVO, SVP, SVX – VHS VCR (mode 3) (pro models)

TA – preamp, amp

TC – cassette deck

 

UP – video printer

 

VCT - tripod

VP* - projector

 

WM – cassette Walkman

WX – Car Stereo

 

XAV – DVD car stereo

XE – car equalizer

XM – car amplifier

XR – Car Stereo

XS – car speaker

 

ZS - Boombox
